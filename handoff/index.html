The mental model

The build has two layers — keep them separate in WordPress. Chrome is shared on every page and belongs in the theme; page content is unique and lives in a fragment; the CSS/JS/images are enqueued once and shared.

→ THEME

Chrome

Header, footer, and the pre-qualify lead form. Injected by chrome.js in the static build; should become the WP theme header/footer + one reusable form block.

See theme-partials ↓

→ PAGE BODY

Content

Everything inside <main> — unique per page. Each content-fragments/*.html is exactly this, with no wrapper, head, header, or footer.

See page inventory ↓

→ ENQUEUE

Assets

One shared site.css + site.js power every page (carousel, accordions, reveals, sticky header, mobile nav, form, dark mode). pages.css for Careers/Tax News; portal.css + portal.js for the portal.

Decisions needed before / during build

  • WordPress approach — custom page template, Gutenberg pattern, or page-builder? Drives the whole estimate. (Q1)
  • What's content-editable vs. hardcoded — hero copy, the 9 service cards, accordion items, the team roster, the guarantee band? (Q2)
  • Where the lead form submits — which form plugin, CRM routing, spam protection. Today it's a front-end demo. (Q3)
  • Tracking & conversion — GTM, GA4, Google Ads, Meta Pixel, CallRail, form-submit event, consent handling. (Q4)
  • SEO structure — keep the landing page's archive semantics (CollectionPage + rel=next, OG "Services Archive") or migrate to WebPage/Service? (Q5)
  • Primary phone number — header (866) 708-2872 vs. footer 888-708-2872. (Q6)
  • Back Sales Tax — author the missing page (copy + slug + video) or keep the card disabled? (Q7)
  • Service-card icons — keep referencing live WP media URLs, or migrate to managed assets? (Q8)
  • Dark-mode toggle & icons8 call GIF — keep in production? Verify the GIF's license. (Q9)
